# BTP-Project
Towards Trustworthy AI for Refurbished Product Price Prediction Using Multimodal Data

Abstract:
The growing demand for affordable and sustainable technologies has strengthened the Refurbished Product (RFP) market. It not only saves costs but also supports recycling and the circular economy. Moreover, determining the price of an RFP remains inherently uncertain due to heterogeneous conditions, incomplete records, and ambiguous evidence from images and textual descriptions. Prior studies have employed various pricing models that rely on regressed price or point predictions. They fail to quantify uncertainty and often yield unreliable, overconfident prices, which reduces trust in the RFP market. This paper proposes a trustworthy, multimodal interval-prediction framework for estimating RFP prices from images and textual descriptions. The framework learns lower and upper price bounds by optimising an interval loss that explicitly models predictive uncertainty. To ensure that the predicted intervals are reliable and informative, we incorporate a tightness constraint that minimises the interval width while preserving coverage of the actual prices. We leverage pretrained CLIP ViT-L/14 encoders to extract aligned 768-dimensional image and text representations, which are fused for interval estimation. Next, due to the unavailability of an RFP dataset, we collect a large-scale dataset of 51,783 refurbished products, each accompanied by images and textual descriptions. We evaluate uncertainty quality using the PICP, MPIW, and CWC. Experimental results demonstrate that our approach yields significantly tighter and more accurate price intervals. By providing interpretable price ranges, our framework enables risk-aware purchasing and improves transparency for trustworthy AI in RFP pricing.
